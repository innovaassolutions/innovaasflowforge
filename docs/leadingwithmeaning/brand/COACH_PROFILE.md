## Voice & Communication Style

### How Mark Communicates
- Direct but empathetic
- Professional but human
- Grounded, not theoretical
- Practical, not abstract
- Hopeful, not fixated on problems

### Sample Language (from website)

**Acknowledging struggle:**
> "You are not struggling. You are leading in chaos without a map, minimal training, and no margin for error."

**Normalizing experience:**
> "Most new leaders aren't given the tools, space, or support to lead well."

**Offering solution:**
> "Stepping into coaching is a meaningful decision, and the first conversation matters."

### Language to Avoid
- Corporate jargon
- Overly academic terminology
- Therapy-speak ("How does that make you feel?")
- Deficit-focused framing ("You have a problem with...")
- Comparison to "successful leaders"
