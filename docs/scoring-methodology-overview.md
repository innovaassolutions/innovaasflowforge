## Multi-Dimensional Analysis

### Beyond Single Scores

Traditional assessments reduce complex realities to a single number. FlowForge takes a fundamentally different approach.

**For Organizations**: We evaluate readiness across three interconnected pillars—Technology, Process, and Organization—each broken into specialized dimensions. This reveals not just where you stand, but why, and what's holding back progress in each area.

**For Individuals**: We map behavioral patterns across multiple contexts—how you lead under pressure, when you're at your best, and what drains your energy. This multi-lens view surfaces insights that single-point assessments miss entirely.

**For Institutions**: We synthesize perspectives across stakeholder groups—identifying not just what's working, but where perceptions diverge and why that matters.

## Evidence-Based Scoring

### Every Score Tells a Story

FlowForge doesn't assign scores in a vacuum. Every rating is anchored to specific evidence:

- **Direct Quotes**: Actual statements from stakeholders that support each finding
- **Pattern Recognition**: Themes that emerge across multiple conversations
- **Confidence Indicators**: Transparency about data quality and stakeholder alignment
- **Gap Analysis**: Clear articulation of what separates current state from the next level

This evidence-first approach means you can trace any score back to its source—and confidently communicate findings to stakeholders who weren't in the room.

## Context-Aware Frameworks

### The Right Tool for Every Assessment

One scoring model doesn't fit all contexts. FlowForge employs purpose-built frameworks for each application:

#### Organizational Readiness
A proven maturity model with six distinct levels (0-5), weighted across Technology (40%), Process (35%), and Organization (25%). Each dimension is scored independently with specific criteria, ensuring nuanced evaluation rather than broad generalizations.

#### Leadership Development
A behavioral archetype system that identifies core leadership patterns across five distinct styles. Rather than labeling leaders as "good" or "bad," we map authentic strengths and surface tension patterns that inform development priorities.

#### Institutional Health
A Four Lenses framework that examines what's holding strong, what's slipping, what's misunderstood across groups, and what's at risk. Triangulation across stakeholder perspectives reveals blind spots that internal assessments often miss.
